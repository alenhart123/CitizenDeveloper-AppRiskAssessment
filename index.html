Four steps every citizen dev app goes through

1
Assess
Run the Risk Assessment tool to understand your app's risk profile — data sensitivity, user scope, platform, connectivity, and output use. IT uses this as the basis for tier assignment.
2
Register
Coming soon
Submit your app registration request. IT reviews it, runs the risk assessment with you if needed, and confirms your governance tier. You receive an Entra security group on approval.
3
Build & deploy
Coming soon
Build your app within the approved platform controls for your tier. Upload the file to the designated SharePoint library. IT reviews, commits to the GitHub repo, and deploys to Azure. You own the app — IT owns the infrastructure.
4
Manage & maintain
Coming soon
Manage your app's Entra group membership to control who has access. Complete the annual self-review each January. If your app's scope grows, re-run the assessment. Transfer ownership or decommission through the hub when the lifecycle ends.

Frequently asked questions
Do I need to register if I am just using an AI tool for my own work?
No. Personal productivity use of approved AI tools -- Microsoft Copilot, approved Claude interfaces -- does not require registration. Registration applies when you build something others will use: an app, automation, or tool with multiple users or business impact.
What platforms are approved for citizen development?
Currently approved: LLM-generated HTML apps, Power Apps, Power Automate, Copilot Studio / Agent Builder (with additional IT review), and SharePoint-hosted tools. Custom code or scripts that require IT to maintain the codebase are not citizen dev -- those are Lane 5 IT development requests. If your platform is not listed, contact your BU IT lead before building.
What is Lane 5 and how is it different from citizen dev?
Lane 5 is a formal IT development request where IT owns and maintains the codebase. Citizen dev apps are built and maintained by the owner -- IT provides infrastructure and governance, not development. If users expect IT to fix bugs or build new features, submit a Lane 5 request through your BU or SRG IT lead instead.
Who determines my governance tier?
IT determines your tier after reviewing your registration request. You can run the Risk Assessment tool yourself to get a sense of where your app will land, but IT's determination is final. IT may run the assessment together with you as part of the review conversation before confirming the tier.
Who reviews my registration and how long does it take?
Your BU or SRG IT lead reviews all registration requests. Tier 2 apps are approved within 5 business days. Tier 3 apps are also reviewed by CenTech for infrastructure provisioning and by Compliance if client data or regulated workflows are involved -- allow up to 10 business days. You will receive updates by email throughout the process.
What does IT provide for Tier 3 apps vs what I am responsible for?
IT owns the infrastructure -- Azure Static Web App, Easy Auth, Entra group provisioning, GitHub deployment, and hosting. You own the app -- its content, logic, updates, and user access management. You are responsible for responding to issues within 5 business days and completing the annual self-review each January.
Can my app connect to external APIs or non-Microsoft data sources?
External connectivity increases your risk score and typically requires Tier 3. All external API connections must be declared in your registration request. IT will confirm whether the connection is approved during their review. Approved MCP servers provided by IT are an alternative to direct external connections for some use cases.
Where is my app tracked after approval?
All registered apps are tracked in the CenTech citizen dev SharePoint registry -- the system of record for ownership, tier, review dates, and compliance status. IT leads and CenTech engineers use this for governance, annual review planning, and provisioning decisions.
What happens if my app's scope grows after registration?
Re-run the Risk Assessment and contact your BU IT lead to discuss re-tiering. The new tier's controls and review requirements apply from that point. You will not lose access to your app during the review unless a compliance issue is identified.
